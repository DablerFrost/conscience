On February 12, 2020, BP's then-CEO Bernard Looney announced the company would "reinvent BP" — cutting oil and gas production by 40% within a decade as part of a net-zero strategy.

"We will have 10 times more renewables capacity, and our oil and gas production will be 40% lower."
— Bernard Looney, BP, 2020

In 2023, BP reversed course. New CEO Murray Auchincloss scaled back the production target following investor pressure. The revised goal: a 25% reduction — itself now widely considered off-track.

In February 2025, BP announced it was abandoning the production reduction target entirely and increasing oil and gas investment. The original pledge is no longer operative.

The numbers in this report are drawn entirely from BP's own public disclosures — their CDP 2024 Climate Response, their SEC mandatory climate disclosure, and their Annual Report 2024. No independent modelling. No estimates.

Key Findings
Finding 01
The pledge was abandoned, not missed.
BP did not simply fail to hit its 40% production reduction target — it formally abandoned the target in February 2025, announcing a strategic reset that increases fossil fuel investment and removes the production reduction commitment entirely. This is not underperformance. It is a reversal. The original 2020 pledge is no longer BP company policy.
Finding 02
38 points behind on a linear path.
On a linear path from 2020 to a 40% reduction by 2030, BP should be at approximately −24% production reduction by now. Verified actual: −2%. The gap is 22 percentage points on the required trajectory — and growing wider as BP increases capital allocation toward oil and gas. There is no announced mechanism to close this gap.
Finding 03
Scope 3 remains the unaddressed 90%.
BP's Scope 3 emissions — produced when customers burn BP's products — represent the dominant share of total lifecycle emissions. These are not covered by any binding reduction target. BP's net zero by 2050 commitment relies on carbon capture and offsets at a scale that has not been demonstrated commercially. The structural gap between pledge and physics has not narrowed.
Finding 04
Investor pressure moved the target, not the emissions.
The sequence is documented in BP's own disclosures: 2020 pledge (40% reduction), 2023 revision under investor pressure (25% reduction), 2025 abandonment (target removed, fossil fuel investment increased). Climate targets that move in response to investor returns rather than physical reality are not accountability infrastructure.
